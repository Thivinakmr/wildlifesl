INTRODUCTION ABOUT SRI LANKAN WILDLIFE

SINHARAJA FOREST RESERVE

Sinharajaya is a rainforest in Sabaragamuwa and Southern Provinces of Sri Lanka. It is full of biodiversity. It is spread over an area of ​​11000 hectares. It consists of a series of narrow ridges and valleys. More than 60% of the trees are endemic to Sinharajaya forest and they are rare. There are more than 50% of endemic animals, birds and butterflies. In 1988, Sinharaja forest was considered a world heritage by UNESCO under the name of Sinharaja reserve. In 1936, Sinharaja forest was identified as the only virgin tropical rain forest in the islanThis forest was considered as a biosphere reserve by Unesco in 1978. The Sinharaja forest is the best place to see flocks of mixed species of birds.(Dsathsarani, 2023)

Udawalawe National Park

Currently Sri Lanka has 26 declared national parks covering 7,444 sq. km (Department of Wildlife Conservation, 2015). Similar to western countries, the main objective in introducing national parks to Sri Lanka was to ensure the maximum protection of wildlife and habitats (Department of Wildlife Conservation, 2015). The management of all these parks is under the central government's Department of Wildlife Conservation. Therefore, all revenue collected - including from issuing entry tickets for visitors and renting park bungalows and vehicles - is transferred to the central government's treasury. Although the revenue from the parks (Fig. 1) is currently running at around Rs. 1.6 billion (approximately 8.9 million US Dollars) per annum.(Kariyawasam et al., 2020)

Hikkaduwa Marine National Park

Hikkaduwa marine national park is one of the three marine national parks in Sri Lanka with rich biodiversity a fringing coral reef. Hikkaduwa marine national park declared as wildlife sanctuary and then upgraded to a nature reserve. Declared as a marine national park in 2002, it boasts of a shallow fringing coral reef with an average depth of around 5 meters, which also reduces coastal erosion by acting as a natural breakwater. Encompassing a total of 101.6 hectares, it lies in the southern province. The park is situated in the wet zone, which makes the relatively dry climate between the southwestern and northeastern monsoons an ideal time for tourists.(Expeditions, n.d.)
